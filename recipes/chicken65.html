Indian Chicken_65

Description

Chicken 65 is a popular Indian spicy fried chicken dish that originated in South India, particularly in Tamil Nadu. It is known for its bold flavors, crispy texture, and vibrant red color, which comes from a mix of red chili powder, yogurt, and other aromatic spices. The dish is typically made by marinating bite-sized chicken pieces in a spicy blend of ginger, garlic, curry leaves, and chilies before being deep-fried to perfection. Some variations also include a tempering of green chilies and curry leaves for an extra burst of flavor.

Widely enjoyed as an appetizer or side dish, Chicken 65 is a favorite at Indian restaurants and street food stalls. While its exact origin is debated, it is believed to have been introduced at the famous Buhari Hotel in Chennai in 1965, giving it its unique name. The dish is often served with lemon wedges and onion slices, making it a deliciously spicy and tangy treat loved by chicken enthusiasts.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Â¼ cup fresh curry leaves, chopped, or more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 drop red food coloring, or as desired
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying, or as needed

Steps

  1. Mix chicken, curry leaves, yogurt, garlic paste, chili powder, coriander, garam masala, and ginger together. Cover and let marinate in a refrigerator for 2 hours.
  2. Add a few drops of red food coloring and mix so that chicken becomes tinted with red.
  3. Heat 2 inches oil in a deep saucepan over medium heat. Lower chicken pieces carefully into the hot oil, working in batches, and fry, stirring frequently, until browned and no longer pink in the centers, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels to drain. Repeat with remaining chicken pieces.
